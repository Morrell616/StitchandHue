History of the Granny Square

The Crochet Granny Square is a beloved and iconic element of the crochet community. It origins trace back to the early 19th century, when women would use small bits of leftover yarn to create the squares, alloweing for resourcefulness in times of scarcity. This practice became especially prevalent during the The Great Depression, as people found ways to make the most out of limited materials. The name "Granny Square" itself evokes a sense of nostalgia, as these squares were often created by grandmothers and passed through generations as cherished handmade gifts.
